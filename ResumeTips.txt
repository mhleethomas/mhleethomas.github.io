* Don’t write a job description.
    * Focus on accomplishments, achievements, and outcomes.
* Include something impressive
* Include skills in context, where possible; keywords are important.
* Your Resume Should Tell a Story
    * Does your content support your story?
    * If you tell a story on your resume with the slightest skill, an objective statement is a complete waste of otherwise useful space.
    * Your resume should convey where you've been and where you are and make it evident where you're going.
    * make your purpose obvious, tell a story, or establish a clear direction.
* an objective statement will state what you hope to achieve, while a summary statement will highlight your accomplishments.
* Every class project is an opportunity to add to your portfolio.
* Open-source projects you can contribute to.
* Build something useless that you're interested in.
* Knowing which tool to use is usually as important as knowing how to use the tool.
* Certifications can help you stand out.
* Look at Courses on Coursera or LinkedIn Learning.
* Conversation Starters
    * What do you do?
    * What are you excited about right now?
    * What is one challenge you're currently facing?
    * What do you value in job candidates?
    * What is something interesting that you've learned recently?
    * What do you like about your current job?
    * What's the coolest project you've worked on recently?
    * What's something about your company that's changed since you started?
* Think back on your career & education. Reflect on 5-10 stories where you encountered a problem and solved it. 
* Identify the situation, the problem, the solution, and the resolution. Write an outline.
* Own your mistakes, and share what you learned from them.
